Tennis History

1877:

The All England Croquet and Lawn Tennis Club holds the first Wimbledon Championship.

1881:

The United States National Lawn Tennis Association (now the USTA) is formed to standardize the rules of tennis.

1896:

Tennis is included in the first modern Olympic Games held in Athens, Greece.

1924:

The International Lawn Tennis Federation (now the ITF) is founded to govern international tennis competitions.

1968:

The Open Era begins, allowing professional players to compete alongside amateurs in major tournaments.

1973:

The ATP (Association of Tennis Professionals) is formed to represent male professional tennis players.

1974:

The WTA (Women's Tennis Association) is founded to represent female professional tennis players.

1984:

Tennis returns to the Olympic Games as a full medal sport in Los Angeles, USA.

2000:

The Sydney Olympics sees the debut of tennis in the Paralympic Games.

2021:

Emma Raducanu becomes the first British woman in 44 years to win a Grand Slam singles title at the US Open.
